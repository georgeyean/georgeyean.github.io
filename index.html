About

I study how globalization, a fundamental transformation of the global and national economies, shapes contemporary political and security environments, including domestic foundations (e.g., democracy, economic security) and international dynamics (e.g., geoeconomic conflicts, autocratic resurgence, the international order). My second research strand focuses on China, including China's rise, U.S.-China relations, and Chinese foreign policy. Overall, my research combines international political economy and security. Particularly, by paying attention to what existing theories may have missed, I seek to offer more comprehensive explanations for today's pressing challenges.

I use a combination of quantitative and qualitative methods, including causal inference, survey methods, textual analysis, machine learning/artificial intelligence, and formal models to uncover the underlying stories. I am affiliated with the Institute for Quantitative Social Sciences (IQSS) at Harvard. I used to work in tech companies such as Cisco Systems as a research engineer on operating systems and data performance.

I believe in the power of political communication and public engagement as a public good.
