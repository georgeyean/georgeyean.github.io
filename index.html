About

I study how globalization, a fundamental transformation of the global economy, shapes contemporary political and security environments, including domestic foundation (e.g., democracy, economic security) and international dynamics (e.g., eonomic conflicts, the U.S.-China relations, the international order). Overall, my research combines international political economy and security, which occasionally touch on technology and innovation, and social norms. Particularly, by paying attention to what existing theories may have missed, I seek to offer more comprehensive explanations for today's pressing challenges. I use a combination of quantitative and qualitative methods, including causal inference, survey method, textual analysis, machine learning, and game theory to uncover the underlying stories.

I am affiliated with the Institute for Quantitative Social Sciences (IQSS) at Harvard. I used to work in tech companies such as Cisco Systems as a research engineer on operating systems and data performance.

I believe in the power of political communication and public engagement as a public good.
